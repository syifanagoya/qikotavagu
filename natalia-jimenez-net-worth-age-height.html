Biography of Natalia Jiménez

Natalia Altea Jiménez Sarmento, professionally known as Natalia Jiménez, was born on December 29, 1981, in Madrid, Spain, to a Spanish father and Portuguese mother, both of whom were musicians. From a young age, N atalia displayed a profound passion for music, and it was evident that she was destined for a remarkable career in the industry.

Natalia’s musical journey began at the tender age of 15 when she started performing in the subway and on the streets of Madrid. Her natural talent and captivating voice quickly garnered attention, setting the stage for what would become a sensational career.

In 2001, Natalia signed an agreement with BMG Entertainment, a pivotal moment that marked the start of her rise to stardom. She became the lead singer of La Quinta Estación, a band with which she would go on to achieve immense success not only in Spain but also in Mexico and the United States. Their albums, including “Flores de Alquiler,” “El Mundo Se Equivoca,” and “Sin Frenos,” catapulted them to the forefront of the music scene.

However, Natalia’s journey was not limited to being the frontwoman of a band. In 2011, she embarked on a solo career with the release of her self-titled album, “Natalia Jiménez.” The album debuted at number one on the iTunes Latin Pop list, solidifying her status as a solo artist. She received a nomination for “Female artist of the year” at the Billboard Latin Music Awards and won the “Revelation of the year” award at the Premio Lo Nuestro in 2012.

Her second solo album, “Creo en Mi,” released in 2015, reached the number one position in Puerto Rico and secured the second spot on the Billboard Latin Albums chart. This album earned her two Latin Grammy nominations for Album of the Year and Song of the Year.

In 2016, Natalia paid tribute to the Mexican-American diva, Jenni Rivera, with her third solo album, “Tributo a la gran señora.”

Natalia Jiménez’s versatility as an artist is further evident in her collaborations with some of the biggest stars in Latin music, including Marc Anthony, Daddy Yankee, and Ricky Martin, among others.

Beyond her music career, Natalia has also ventured into television, serving as a coach on various seasons of “La Voz Kids USA” and “La Voz…Mexico.” Her talent and expertise in the industry have made her a sought-after figure in talent shows.

In 2019, Natalia released the single “Nunca Es Tarde,” a collaboration with Jesús Navarro of Reik, which became the main song of the second season of the Televisa and Univision series, “Amar Sin Ley.”

One of Natalia’s remarkable achievements came in August 2019 when she released the album “México de mi corazón,” a collection of rancheras. This album, featuring duets with prominent voices like Carlos Rivera, Pedro Fernández, Paquita la del Barrio, Lila Downs, El Bebeto, and Banda MS, garnered massive success in Mexico and the United States.

Natalia Jiménez’s passion for music extends beyond the stage as she has played a role in nurturing young talent. She has served as a judge and coach on various reality shows, including “Operación Triunfo” in Spain and the Colombian Kids and Senior versions of “La Voz,” where she emerged victorious.

While her professional life has been nothing short of extraordinary, Natalia’s personal life has seen its share of ups and downs. She had a wedding scheduled with her fiancé, businessman Antonio Alcol, in 2009, which was canceled, leading to their separation. In 2016, she surprised the world with her wedding to her longtime boyfriend and manager, Daniel Trueba. The couple has a daughter together, born in 2016. However, in January 2021, Natalia announced their separation after several months of being separated.

Natalia Jiménez’s life is a testament to her unwavering dedication to her craft and her ability to connect with audiences through her powerful voice and heartfelt lyrics. Her contributions to Latin music have earned her numerous awards and accolades, and her journey continues to inspire both aspiring artists and dedicated fans worldwide.
