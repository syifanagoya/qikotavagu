Early Life & Biography

George Eads was born on 1st March 1967, in Fort Worth, Texas, U.S., to Author Coleman Eads and Vivian Baker. He has only one sibling, Angela Eads Tekell. He completed his schooling from Belton High School. Later, he went to Texas Tech University, where he earned a degree in marketing. During his college days, he was a member of the Phi Delta Theta fraternity.

Eads did various odd jobs before pursuing a career in acting, including copy machine salesman. He also used to sell first aid and safety supplies for Cintas.

Career

Eads borrowed a pickup truck from his stepfather and drove to Los Angeles, California to pursue his acting career. There he could only drive his truck during day time because he had two broken headlights.

Eads got his first break on the prime time opera soap Savannah, but his character died in the first episode. He became so popular that the producers started using him as a flashback and eventually bought him back as the character’s identical twin.

Later, he worked on several television films like Crowned and Dangerous in 1997, Monte Walsh in 2003, Evel Knievel in 2004.

In 2000, Eads portrayed the character of the forensic scientist Nick Stokes and became one of the lead characters of the CBS police drama CSI: Crime Scene Investigation. In this police drama series, he worked many years, but in the 14th season in 2013, he had a conflict with one of the writers, and he left the show.

In 2016, due to his popularity on the show, Eads was again starred as Jack Dalton in CBS’s new version of the MacGyver show. In 2018 he asked the producer of the show for some time-off and left because he wanted to spend some time with the daughter, but due to his popularity on the show, the producer left open the opportunity for him to return.
