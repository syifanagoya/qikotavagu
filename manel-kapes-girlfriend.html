Manel Kape, an Angolan-Portuguese MMA fighter born on November 14, 1993, now fights in the UFC's Flyweight division.

Kape has been fighting professionally since 2012, previously competing for the Rizin Fighting Federation, Cage Fighters, and Knock Out Championship. He had previously held the Rizin and Knock Out Championship bantamweight titles.

Kape's first MMA fight occurred when he was 14 years old. He faced Artur Gomes in his first professional bout at the age of 17 while competing with the Cage Fighters at Cage Fighters 2. Kape was victorious in the first round with a TKO.

Kape took a two-year sabbatical from boxing due to the three postponed contests. He later joined the Rizin Fighting Federation and was set to compete in the Rizin bantamweight Grand Prix. When he took on Erson Yamamoto at Rizin 6, he made his debut.

Yamamoto was confused by Kape's stated comment during the fight that "[Your] mom just lost and [You] are next," given that Erson's mother had already lost earlier in the evening. Despite the fact that many observers thought the stoppage was premature, Kape went on to win the fight with a left-leg head kick KO after only 71 seconds.

On March 30, 2020, the Ultimate Fighting Championship announced that Kape had signed a four-fight contract with them, making him the first Portuguese-Angolan fighter to do so.

Kape was scheduled to make his UFC 252 debut on August 15, 2020, against Rogério Bontorin. Due to Bontorin's ankle injury, the fight was later called off.

Kape was scheduled to fight Deiveson Figueiredo on July 8, 2023, at UFC 290. Figueiredo withdrew from the fight owing to a lack of medical clearance.

Kape and Kai Kara-France were scheduled to fight on September 10, 2023, at UFC 293. Felipe dos Santos, a promotional newcomer, was brought in to replace Kara-France, who backed out of the bout in late August due to an injury.
